Your Story

Your business relies heavily on you, limiting its appeal to buyers.

Ellison Dynamics thrives under your leadership, but its reliance on your direct involvement raises questions for potential buyers. While the business shows strong margins and consistent revenue, the perception of owner-dependency could temper its valuation or deter interest.

After two decades of building this, how might you shift its identity from being your business to a business that stands independently?

Excess retained cash could jeopardise tax relief on the sale.

The profitability of your company has led to a buildup of cash on the balance sheet, but this surplus risks disqualifying the business from Business Asset Disposal Relief. Without a clear strategy for managing these retained earnings, the tax implications could significantly impact the proceeds of your exit.

Is there a way to align your financial goals with the rules that preserve this relief?

Inheritance Tax exposure could erode the wealth meant for your family.

Post-sale, liquid assets from your exit will lose protection under Business Property Relief, leaving them exposed to significant Inheritance Tax. With no estate planning yet in place, the risk to your family's future grows as wealth transitions from the business to personal assets.

How might you balance supporting your children's housing needs with preserving the legacy you've worked to build?

What to Discuss With Your Adviser

These are the must-know topics for your next conversation, in priority order.

  1. Business heavily reliant on owner, reducing appeal to buyers and valuation potential

    Your business is heavily dependent on your hands-on involvement, which could deter buyers or reduce its value. Addressing owner-dependency now can materially improve your exit outcome.

  2. Excess retained cash on balance sheet risks QSBS eligibility

    Retained cash sitting on the balance sheet could jeopardise Business Asset Disposal Relief eligibility. Ensuring cash is actively used in the business needs to be addressed before any sale process begins.

  3. Significant IHT exposure on post-sale assets without proper estate planning

    After the sale, the business will no longer benefit from Business Property Relief, leaving your estate exposed to substantial Inheritance Tax liabilities.

  4. Concentration risk with wealth tied predominantly to the business

    The majority of your wealth is tied up in the business. A successful exit will provide an opportunity to diversify and build a more resilient financial position.
