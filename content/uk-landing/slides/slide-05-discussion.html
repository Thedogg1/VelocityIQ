For Your Meeting

What to Discuss With Your Adviser

These are the must-know topics for your next conversation, in priority order.

  • 1

    Business heavily reliant on owner, reducing appeal to buyers and valuation potential

    Your business is heavily dependent on your hands-on involvement, which could deter buyers or reduce its value. Addressing owner-dependency now — by delegating key relationships and decisions — can materially improve your exit outcome.

  • 2

    Excess retained cash on balance sheet risks BADR eligibility

    The retained cash sitting on your balance sheet, while a result of profitability, could jeopardise your Business Asset Disposal Relief eligibility. Ensuring the cash is actively used in the business — or distributed appropriately — needs to be addressed before any sale process begins.

  • 3

    Significant IHT exposure on post-sale assets without proper estate planning

    After the sale, the business will no longer benefit from Business Property Relief, leaving your estate exposed to substantial Inheritance Tax liabilities. Early estate planning is essential to protect the wealth you are working to release.

  • 4

    Concentration risk with wealth tied predominantly to the business

    The majority of your wealth is tied up in the business, creating a significant concentration risk. A successful exit will provide an opportunity to diversify your holdings and build a more resilient financial position for the long term.

  • 5

    Emotional uncertainty about retirement and life after the sale

    While you are clearly focused on the financial aspects of an exit, there appears to be some uncertainty about what life will look like after the sale. Retirement planning that goes beyond the numbers — addressing purpose, identity, and lifestyle — will be an important part of the conversation.
